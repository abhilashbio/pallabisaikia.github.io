ABOUT DAYASAGAR

BRIEF HISTORY OF DAYASAGAR

“DAYASAGAR” is a Premier State Level Voluntary Non-Government Organization of Assam was registered on 4th June, 2001 under Societies Registration Act XXI of 1860, vide Registration No RS/ KAM/ 240/ L/ 102 of 2001-2002. As the Society is a Socio-Economic Voluntary Organization, it has been working since its inception i.e. 2001 in the field of all-round development of poor and downtrodden people, particularly women, in pursuance of the Main Aim and Objectives for which the Society was constituted. The Year 2014-15 is a year full of activities particularly in the field of Training for different sectors. The most important job carried out during the year was up-gradation of the existing Training Institute, namely “DAYASAGAR INSTITUTE OF TRAINING” (DIT) into a full-fledged modern Training Institute. Satisfied with our infrastructure and convinced about our core competencies in different sectors, various Government Departments have entrusted us different works involving Capacity building and Training of Grass-root workers & others covering all the districts of the state during the year.

UPGRADING OF THE TRAINING INSTITUTE

“DAYASAGAR INSTITUTE OF TRAINING” (DIT) was upgraded into a Training Institute of excellence by expansion & renovations of the old building into a four storey R.C.C building and adding many infrastructure facilities to impart any Training Programme competently and effectively. The present Institute of Training has 3 (Three) Training Halls to accommodate more than 120 trainees at a time @ 40 trainees in each of the Training halls. The Training Halls has been made spacious and equipped with good modern seating facilities and Audio-visual system is also incorporated in the Training Halls to impart the training effectively. Besides, the Training Institute has Reception, Library, and Instructors’ room, Conference Hall, Offices of various Executives, well-equipped Laboratory and Workshop. The Training Institute is also equipped with 6 no of computers, and other related training supporting equipment and fixtures to make the Post-Training Report and other office related works. The Institute has a Library having books on wide-ranging subjects, which is expected to benefit the trainees as well as our staffs immensely. This Institute has good lodging and fooding facilities initially for 40 trainees, which would be increased in near future. The Institute has now a well- equipped Kitchen with Dining hall which can cater good food with tea at the required time. The Training Section is headed by Chief Training Manager having 34 years of experience working in various reputed organizations in India and abroad. He is an Engineer with an Executive MBA from abroad. He is supported by a team of qualified and experienced faculties in various disciplines. Further, the Institute has good supporting manpower to carry out the activities in a result-oriented manner. Focusing on “Hands on” Practical Training, the Institute has also installed 3 Improved Looms with Jacquard facilities for conducting Handloom Weaving training. Similarly, for Water & Sanitation Training, a model “Low cost Toilet” has been constructed to impart training effectively to trainees of Water & Sanitation. Further, it has a Laboratory to test water samples collected from different parts of the state.

WATER & SANITATION TRAINING PROGRAMMES

Water & Sanitation Support Organization (WSSO), under Public Health Engineering Department, Govt. of Assam has entrusted us the following three Training Programmes :

i. Training for Grassroots workers on Water Quality Issues, Water Testing through Field Testing Kit (FTK), vials etc. - 10,000 No Trainees (covering all districts) -1 day training of each batch. Water Quality Issues & Water Testing Training Programme (all the three training programmes) was inaugurated on 2nd February, 2015 in presence of Director, WSSO, PHED & other officials of WSSO and Director, Social Welfare department, Govt. of Assam in our DAYASAGAR Institute of Training (DIT). The Training Module was designed and accordingly training was imparted both in theory & practical batch by batch and was completed within the target date of 31st March 2015. As this project was completed within a very short period of 2 months, the WSSO, PHED is highly satisfied and has committed another training of higher targets from next year.

ii. Training of Grassroot workers for Mason -1500 no Trainees (covering all districts) – 2 days training of each batch. The Training was imparted both theory & practical training batch by batch and was completed within the target date of 31st March 2015. As this project is also completed within a very short period of 2 months, the WSSO, PHED appreciated our works and has promised another Training Programme of higher targets from next year.

iii. Training of Pump Mechanics - 960 no Trainees (covering all districts) - 1 day training of each batch. The Training was imparted both theory & practical training batch by batch and was completed within the target date of 31st March 2015. As this project is completed within a very short period of 2 months, the WSSO, PHED is praised our performance and has assured us of giving another Training Programme of higher targets from next year.


TRAINING PROGRAMME ON HANDLOOM WEAVING

Social Welfare Department, Govt. of Assam, after assessing our infrastructure & other facilities, have sanctioned a Training Programme on Handloom Weaving - 2,830 nos. of SHGs (covering all districts) -7 day training of each batch. Starting on 9th March, 2015, the Training was imparted both in theory & practical batch by batch with ‘hands-on’ in our 3 no of modern handlooms with jacquard facilities installed in our institute through competent In-house Trainers and Guest faculty. This training will continue throughout the next year. Further, several projects by the Organization have been submitted to different Central Government Departments which are under consideration. The NGO has a full-fledged Training Institute is headed by a person having good experience working in various reputed organizations in Assam. He is supported by a team of qualified and experienced persons. The “DAYASAGAR” has adequate competent manpower to carry out other administrative and financial activities of the organization with well laid out policy. Our strength lies in team efforts which make us possible to carry out any assignments.


OBJECTIVES

The main objectives of the proposed project can be summed up as under :
1. Promotion of Disability Care services in the areas covered by organizing the poor disabled people and motivating them to take up aids/appliances to maintain their activities, particularly economic ones.
2. Disability alleviation and elevation of status of poor & backward disabled people through improved means of aids and appliances through this scheme.
3. Providing quality and certified aids and appliances.
4. To encourage the concerned employees to be more dedicated and result-oriented in their services.
5. To mobilize the disabled people to take up /avail Government Programmes for the economic up-liftment.


STRATEGY

The following strategies will be adopted to achieve the objectives of the scheme.
1. Adoption of modernized equipment and selection of expert professionals for prescription of the required artificial aids/appliances.
2. Maintaining of records for the beneficiaries with their proper addresses and contact no for future requirement.
3. Imparting training to the employees to make them conversant with latest technology and other intricacies of disability management.
4. Based on feedback from the disabled people, proper action will be taken for up-gradation of post-fitment facilities
5. Adoption of separate account for this project and submitting the Utilization Certification in time to the concerned authority.
